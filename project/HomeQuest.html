Section 06

Honest answers to the obvious questions.

What you'd ask if we were on the phone. Same answers, written down. If yours isn't here, write to info@hqhousing.com — Philippe replies in under four hours during business hours.

Why €250 upfront? Other agents are free.
Because the people who work for free are paid by the landlord. That's who they actually represent — not you. €250 is the cost of having someone whose only job is to look out for your interests. It's also a filter: it keeps us focused on clients who are genuinely ready to move, which is what allows us to deliver a shortlist in five days instead of five weeks.
What happens if you don't find me a home?
If we haven't placed you in a home you accept within 5 weeks, the €250 is refunded in full on request. Decide to stop the search before then? Same answer — you only ever pay the €250. No cure, no pay, no clawback, no admin fee. Written into the service agreement you sign before we begin.
Is there an actual contract?
Yes. A two-page service agreement signed by both of us before any payment. It specifies the deliverables, the timeline, the refund terms and the per-property success fee for buying (rentals are €250 flat). HomeQuest operates as a Spanish S.L. registered in Valencia — CIF B-98432891, inscribed in the Registro Mercantil de Valencia, with EU VAT ESB98432891. You can verify all three on the Spanish business registry before paying anything.
How long does it typically take?
The average across 312 matches is 21 days from intake to accepted offer. Rentals are usually faster (7–18 days), purchases slower (4–6 weeks). The fastest was 4 days; the longest was 73. We tell you on the intake call which side of average to expect for your specific brief.
Do you work with expats arriving from abroad?
About 70% of our clients are arriving from outside the Netherlands. Intake calls run on your timezone, viewings can be done by video, and contracts are translated clause-by-clause into English (or French / Spanish). We've worked with relocations to ASML, Booking, Optiver, ESA, the ICC, and a long list of smaller employers.
What's the difference between this and a regular makelaar?
A listing makelaar represents the seller or landlord. An aankoopmakelaar represents you, but usually only on the legal/transactional side — they don't curate, they don't negotiate aggressively on rentals, and they don't help you settle in. We sit one layer above: we handle the search, the strategy, the negotiation and the human bits, and we work with a licensed makelaar for the legal closing when needed.
Are there extra fees beyond the €250?
For rentals, the placement fee is one month's rent + VAT for clients with a straightforward profile, or 1.5 months' rent + VAT if a guarantor is needed (students, freelancers, income verified abroad), with a minimum of €1,250 + VAT. The €250 onboarding is fully deducted from whichever applies. Over 83% of the payment only happens once you've signed the lease — no cure, no pay. For purchases there is a 1% success fee (capped at €7,500), payable only when the keys change hands.
